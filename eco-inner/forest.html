Forest

The Amazon Rainforest | Brazil

The Amazon has a very rich ecosystem. It covers 2% of the Earth's surface, but it is home to 50% of plant and animal life. It is covered with thousands of plants, animals, and new life being discovered every year!

The Gir National Park | India

Many African animals are found in the Gir National Park. The last Asiatic lion in all of Asia lives here. As well as, leopards, jackets, hyenas, antelopes, and much more!

The Daintree Rainforest | Australia

These forests are some of the oldest in the world at 180 million years old. Scientists believe that dinosaurs roamed these same forests millions of years ago. This is why many dinosaur documentaries use the Daintree Rainforest as filming sets for dinosaur documentaries or movies.

The Arashiyama | Japan

Built-in the 14th century in honor of the Japanese emperor, filled with not only bamboo but also cherry blossoms and all types of trees. It changes year-round for tourists and people who wanna see the extraordinary beauty of Japan.

The Yellow Stone National Park | USA

Yellow Stone is larger than the states Rhode Island and Deleware put together. There are 1,000 miles of Nature trails in Yellow Stone. Tourists can spot many of the USA's most incredible animals such as bears, wolves, eagles, and foxes.
