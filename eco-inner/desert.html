Deserts

The Moab Desert | USA

Called the “biological soil crust”, it covers much of the land. It is composed of algae, lichens and, bacteria, this crust provides a secure foundation for desert plants. A living desert!

The Northeastern Sahara Desert | Egypt

The Sahara is home to surprisingly a variety of wildlife that has adapted to a life of extremes. Camels and goats have adpated but, cheetahs, gazelles, ostriches, Fennec foxes and monitor lizards.

The Northwestern Sahara Desert | Morocco

The Sahara Desert is the largest hot desert in the world that spans 11 countries. The desert is about the size of the USA and growing every year!

The Outback | Australia

The Outback can rain a lot some years and never rain at all other years. Since the desert is dry and unique, it has fossilized dinosaur footprints.

The Kalahari Desert| South Africa

Unlike, most deserts that stay hot all year, the Kalahari Desert is very hot in the summer, and very cold in the winters. This leads to animals showing up at different times depending on the season.
